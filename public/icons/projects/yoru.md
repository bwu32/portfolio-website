I created this 1:1 scale replica of Yoru’s mask from *VALORANT* as a Secret Santa gift.

## Digital Prep & Printing

The first challenge with any wearable prop is the fit. I spent some time accurately sizing the 3D model to my own face measurements to ensure it would be comfortable for the recipient. 

- **Orientation Strategy:** I chose a print orientation that prioritized surface quality on the front face while minimizing the need for supports. This saves hours of cleanup in the post-processing phase.
- **Surface Prep:** After printing, I began the rigorous "Sand-Prime-Sand" cycle, using automotive filler primer & bondo glazing and spot putty to eliminate the 3D print layer lines and create a perfectly smooth substrate for the metallic coats.

## Turning a Fail into a Win: The Paint Cycle

Even with years of experience, environment matters. I initially painted the base metallic silver in conditions that were too cold, resulting in an "orange peel" finish. 

- **The Pivot:** Instead of stripping the paint, I realized the primary color of the mask was blue. I used the silver layer as a high-durability "undercoat," sanding back the orange peel and using the blue layer to smooth out the finish.
- **Taping & Detailing:** I carefully taped off sections of the mask to allow the silver to shine through as "exposed metal." Once the blue was set, I masked it again to apply the vibrant orange highlights, ensuring clean, sharp lines between colors.

## Final Touches & Weathering

The difference between a "toy" and a "prop" is the grime. To achieve a photorealistic look, I applied a heavy **Blackwash** (a 2:1 mix of water and black acrylic paint).

- **Weathering:** I slathered the mask in the wash and quickly wiped it away, leaving the dark pigment in the crevices and "damaged" areas to simulate field use.
- **Wearability:** For the final assembly, I integrated a medical-grade mask on the underside to act as a breathable black mesh for the mouth and added elastic straps for a secure, all-day fit.

`The Result:` A gift that looked like it was pulled straight out of a Riot Games cinematic. Despite the environmental painting issues, the final piece successfully demonstrated my ability to troubleshoot on the fly and deliver a professional-grade finish.
