This project was five years in the making. Back in 2020, my friend and I designed "Original Characters" (OCs) within the *Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba* universe, complete with custom katanas and unique Haori patterns. For Otakon 2025, we finally decided to bring those sketches to life.

## The Designs: Concept vs. Reality

Our original visions were ambitious, but the "con-crunch" forced us to iterate on the fly to meet our deadline.

- **The "Firefly" Haori:** Originally designed as a lavender-to-indigo ombre with glowing orange "firefly" dots. Due to time and material constraints, I pivoted to a light-blue-to-dark-blue gradient with white stars. This kept the "night sky" spirit while being achievable with the fabric available.
- **The "Cloud" Haori:** A sleek, primarily black design featuring subtle gray gradient swirl patterns on the sleeves to evoke a misty, cloud-like aesthetic.

## 48 Hours to Showtime

I sewed both Haoris in the two nights immediately preceding the convention—one full garment per night. This was my first experience working with heavy, drapey garments and wide-set sleeves, which provided a steep learning curve.

- **The Brocade Battle:** For the black Haori, I used a beautiful but unforgiving brocade. I hit a snag with the sleeve instructions, and because brocade is notoriously difficult to seam-rip without destroying the weave, I had to "cut my losses" literally (I shortened the sleeves at the seam) and adapt the design mid-build.
- **Fabric Logistics:** I learned a hard lesson in checking fabric widths. I assumed the brocade was 60" wide, but it arrived at only 30", forcing me to scavenge cosplay fabric to finish the piece.
- **Upcycling for Comfort:** For the blue Haori, I used a comfortable cotton poplin blend for the shell and upcycled an old bedsheet for the lining. This made it much more breathable for a long day on the convention floor.

## Lessons in Drape & Construction

- **Sleeve Geometry:** Haori sleeves are deceptively complex. Figuring out how they attach to the body while maintaining that iconic "boxy" drape was a challenge, especially when working under pressure.
- **Material Choice:** This project taught me the importance of fabric weight. The stiffness of the brocade vs. the softness of the poplin changed how each character felt and moved.
- **Dye is Hard:** I folded my haori sleeves pieces because they were way too big when I was dyeing them. Unfortunately, this resulted in the dye not soaking evenly into my pieces resulting in an almost boxy, sectioned appearance for the gradient. I also spilled/splashed dye on parts that were supposed to be clear, resulting in stains on the back and a bit of the front. Once it dried it ultimately didn't matter too much because it just contributed to the design. In the future I will be sure to fold it less times.

`The Retrospective:` Despite the shortened sleeves on the black model and the modified colors on the blue, wearing our 2020 designs in 2025 was incredibly rewarding. I’m already planning a "V2" where I can custom-dye the indigo ombre and hand-embroider the fireflies to truly match the original vision.
