I wanted to create a garment that sat at the intersection of traditional Chinese heritage and modern "streetwear" athletics. I took inspiration from the classic silhouette of the **Chinese Tang shirt** and the high-energy design of **Adidas Chinese New Year** exclusive jackets. Instead of an athletic jacket, I opted for an oversized jacket that is typically worn over hoodies.

## The Engineering of the Garment

Unlike a standard shirt, this jacket required significant hardware integration and structural planning.

- **The Hidden Zipper:** I wanted the utility of a zipper with the clean look of a traditional button-up. This required me to learn how to cut a **separating zipper** to a custom length, modify the end-teeth for a clean stop, and install it so the pull remained smooth yet primarily hidden behind the placket.
- **Magnetic Utility:** To modernize the piece, I swapped traditional pocket buttons for **magnetic button clasps**. This gives the pockets a "snap-to-close" feel that is much more functional for everyday use.
- **Cultural Detailing:** I integrated traditional **frog closure knots** on the front and as cufflinks on the sleeves. While they provide a great visual "pop," this prototype taught me that higher-quality, hand-made closures will be necessary for V2 to match the weight of the fabric.

## Material & Fit Challenges

Working with **Cotton Canvas** provided a rugged, structured look, but it came with a steep learning curve regarding "drape."

- **The Stiffness Factor:** I realized that heavy canvas can be unforgiving, especially in high-movement areas like the armpits. The jacket currently lacks the "flow" of a traditional Tang shirt, though I expect the fitment to improve as the fibers soften with prolonged wear.
- **Sleeve Geometry:** This being a "test model," I unfortunately cut the sleeves slightly too short. However, this provided a valuable lesson in accounting for "take-up"—how much fabric is lost in the seams and bends of the arm.
- **The High Collar:** I successfully executed the signature high collar of the Tang jacket, providing a stiff, professional frame for the neck that holds its shape thanks to the canvas weight.



## Retrospective & V2 Goals

This project wasn't just about sewing; it was about **technical problem-solving**. 
- **Next Time:** I plan to tackle this project again using a slightly lighter weight fabric or a pre-washed duck canvas for better movement. 
- **Custom Hardware:** I intend to hand-tie my own frog closures from scratch to ensure the quality matches the rest of the build.

`Lessons Learned:` Measuring twice and cutting once applies to fabric just as much as it does to wood. Even with the sleeve and stiffness issues, this prototype proved that I can successfully integrate complex hardware into a wearable, culturally-significant design.
